Lost in the Middle

AI recall is a U-curve, not a flat line.

Position of fact in prompt →
  • Sharp at the start. First ~10K tokens get full attention.
  • Sharp at the end. Last ~5K tokens (recency).
  • Hazy in the middle. Recall drops 20 to 40% on facts buried mid-prompt.
  • ▶Documented in Liu et al. 2023, "Lost in the Middle" and reproduced on every frontier model since.

Same prompt. Same model. Same question. Move the answer to the middle, and it gets it wrong.

Build Your LLM Team WikiModule 2 · Context Mechanics
1:45. This is the slide. Get this and you stop fighting your AI. Researchers at Stanford in 2023 ran a clean experiment: bury the answer to a question at different positions in a long prompt. Then ask the question. What they found, and what every frontier model still shows today, is a U-curve. The AI is sharp at the beginning. It is sharp at the end. And it goes hazy in the middle. Recall drops between 20 and 40 percent on facts buried in the middle. Same prompt, same model, same question, just move the answer. So when you dump "read my entire Google Drive" into one chat, the stuff that matters is sitting in the dead zone. This is not a model getting smarter problem. It is a how you load context problem.
